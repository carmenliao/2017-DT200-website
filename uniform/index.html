Introduction

"At Wellington East Girls' College, we are proud of our place in the education community."

It's important that students are prepared for learning, and to do so, they are required to have correct uniform. There are 2 different sets of uniform. Students are expected to wear the correct uniform sets, depending on what term it is and also for special occasions. The two sets of uniform are:

  • Summer uniform
  • Winter uniform

Sometimes, students are required to wear a 'formal' set of uniform. However, this is usually only worn on formal occasions, such as the school prizegiving.

    WEGC DOES NOT allow jewellery, nail polish and certain earrings to be worn.

    However, despite WEGC having a uniform, the school still advocates individuality, and encourages students to 'be themselves'. For example, students are allowed to dye their hair, and have any hairstyle they want.

    "We are proud of the reputation we have for student centred learning based on valuing the unique diversity of each student."
